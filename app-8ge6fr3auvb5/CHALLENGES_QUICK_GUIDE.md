## The "Group" Concept

When we say **"group challenges"**, we mean:

- **Group** = All the people who joined the challenge
- **Challenge** = The shared habit goal everyone is working on
- **Participants** = Members of the group

### Example:

**"Join group challenges"** means:
- Join challenges where OTHER PEOPLE are also participating
- Be part of a GROUP working on the same habit
- Stay accountable with your GROUP members

## How It Works

### Step 1: Someone Creates a Challenge
```
Sarah creates: "Morning Meditation Challenge"
- Habit: Meditate 10 minutes
- Duration: 21 days
- Status: Open for anyone to join
```

### Step 2: People Join (Form a Group)
```
Participants join:
1. Sarah (creator)
2. John
3. Emma
4. Mike
5. Lisa
... and more

Now you have a GROUP of people!
```

### Step 3: Everyone Tracks Progress
```
Each person in the GROUP:
- Completes the habit daily
- Tracks their days completed
- Sees how many others are in the group
- Stays motivated by group accountability
```

### Step 4: Challenge Ends
```
After 21 days:
```

## Why Use Challenges?

### 1. Group Accountability
- You're not alone
- Others are counting on you
- Group motivation keeps you going

### 2. Social Support
- See how many people are with you
- Feel part of a community
- Shared commitment to the goal

### 3. Friendly Competition
- Compare progress with others
- Push yourself to keep up
- Celebrate together

### 4. Higher Success Rate
- Groups have better completion rates
- Accountability increases commitment
- Social pressure (positive) helps

## Common Questions

### Q: What does "group challenges" mean?
**A:** Challenges where multiple people (a group) participate together.

### Q: How many people are in a group?
**A:** It varies! Could be 2 people or 100+ people. You'll see the participant count.

### Q: Can I create my own group?
**A:** Yes! Create a challenge, and anyone who joins becomes part of your group.

### Q: Do I have to interact with group members?
**A:** No direct interaction required. The group provides accountability through shared participation.

### Q: Can I join multiple groups?
**A:** Yes! Join as many challenges as you want.

### Q: What if I'm the only one in a group?
**A:** You can still complete the challenge solo. Others might join later!

## Quick Actions

### To Create a Group Challenge:
1. Go to Challenges page
2. Click "Create Challenge"
3. Fill in details
4. Submit
5. Wait for others to join your group!

### To Join a Group Challenge:
1. Go to Challenges page
2. Browse "All Challenges"
3. Find one you like
4. Click "Join Challenge"
5. You're now part of the group!

### To View Your Groups:
1. Go to Challenges page
2. Click "My Challenges" tab
3. See all challenges you've joined
4. Track your progress in each group
